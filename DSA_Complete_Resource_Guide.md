# PHASE 1 - FOUNDATIONS

## Topic 1: Time & Space Complexity (Big-O)
**What to understand in detail:** What O(1), O(log n), O(n), O(n log n), O(n²), O(2ⁿ) actually mean; how to count operations in single/nested loops; best/average/worst case; why we drop constants; space complexity including recursion stack space.
- 🎥 WATCH: Striver's "Time and Space Complexity" lecture - first video of A2Z Step 1 (open Step 1 in the sheet). For a deeper mathematical take: Abdul Bari's early videos on his channel (Asymptotic Notations).
- 📖 READ: NeetCode's free Big-O guide inside "How to prepare for coding interviews": https://blog.neetcode.io/p/prepare-coding-interviews - includes a Big-O comparison chart.
- ✏️ PRACTICE: No coding problems needed - instead, for every problem you solve from now on, *write down its time & space complexity before submitting*.
- ✅ SOLUTIONS: Check your complexity answer against the editorial of each problem.

## Topic 2: Basic Math (digits, GCD, primes)
**What to understand in detail:** Extract digits with %10 and /10; reverse a number; palindrome number; Armstrong numbers; print all divisors in O(√n); check prime in O(√n); Euclid's GCD algorithm and *why* gcd(a,b) = gcd(b, a mod b).
- 🎥 WATCH: A2Z Step 1 → "Basic Maths" lecture (sheet row has the video).
- 📖 READ: CP-Algorithms → "Euclidean algorithm for computing the greatest common divisor" and "Primality tests" (Algebra section): https://cp-algorithms.com

## Topic 3: Recursion (basics)

# PHASE 5 - DP, TRIES, ADVANCED STRINGS

## Topic 20: Dynamic Programming
**What to understand in detail (the full arc):**
1. Recursion → **memoization** (top-down) → **tabulation** (bottom-up) → **space optimization** - do all 4 for every early problem.
2. **1D DP:** climbing stairs, frog jump, house robber.
3. **Grid DP:** unique paths, min path sum, triangle.
4. **Subset/knapsack DP:** subset sum, partition equal subset, **0/1 knapsack**, coin change (the difference between combinations and permutations orders), target sum, unbounded knapsack, rod cutting.
5. **String DP:** LCS and its whole family (print LCS, longest common substring, shortest common supersequence, min insertions/deletions), **edit distance**, distinct subsequences, wildcard matching.
6. **Stock DP:** all 5 buy-sell variants (state machines).
7. **LIS:** O(n²) DP and the O(n log n) patience/binary-search method; printing the LIS.
8. **Partition DP / MCM:** matrix chain multiplication, burst balloons, palindrome partitioning II.
9. **DP on squares:** maximal square/rectangle.
- 🎥 WATCH: Striver's dedicated **DP playlist (~56 videos)** - find it on the takeUforward channel playlists page: https://www.youtube.com/c/takeuforward/playlists (it's the "Dynamic Programming" playlist; also linked row-by-row inside A2Z Step 16). Alternative angle: **Aditya Verma's DP playlist** (search his channel on YouTube) - beloved for pattern-recognition teaching ("this is just knapsack in disguise").
- 📖 READ: CPH book Chapter 7 "Dynamic programming"; CP-Algorithms DP articles; row editorials (each shows memo → tab → space-optimized).
- ✏️ PRACTICE: A2Z Step 16 (~56 rows) + NeetCode "1-D DP" (12) and "2-D DP" (11).

# 📌 The daily loop (this is the whole method)

1. Open the current A2Z sheet section → watch **one** concept video.
2. Close the video. Code it from memory. Fail. Rewatch the part you forgot. This struggle IS the learning.
3. Solve that row's practice problems: try **20–30 min on your own** → if stuck, read/watch the solution → understand it → **close it and re-code from scratch**.
4. Say the time & space complexity out loud.
5. Check the row's box. Star anything shaky for weekly revision (the sheet has a Revision tab).
6. When a concept won't click: VisuAlgo animation → Abdul Bari's video → CP-Algorithms article. Three different angles fix almost everything.

> Rule of thumb from NeetCode's own guide: looking at solutions is **not cheating** - spending 3 hours stuck on one problem is just slow. Look after ~25 minutes, understand deeply, re-code from scratch, and revisit the problem a week later.
